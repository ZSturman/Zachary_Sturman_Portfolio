Data Analytics

What is "Data Analytics"?


"Data analytics converts raw data into actionable insights. It includes a range of tools, technologies, and processes used to find trends and solve problems by using data. Data analytics can shape business processes, improve decision-making, and foster business growth."

That is a pretty general definition, and rightly so. Data Analysis is being integrated into nearly every business out there so the forms this position can take on change drastically from industry to industry.

How is it done?

With so many different platforms, tools and use-cases it can be hard to find the correct system to use.

These steps can be iterative and may be repeated multiple times to refine the analysis and reach a better understanding of the data.

BUSINESS

Time to ask questions! Figure out what we want to know or what question we want to answer with the data.

DATA

Gather all the relevant data you need to answer your question. Surveys, questioneers, video or audio analysis, sales data, etc.

PREP

Get the data ready by cleaning, organizing and transforming.

ANALYSIS

Use various methods to examine the data and find patterns, relationships, and insights.

VISUALIZE

Present the results of your analysis in a way that's easy to understand, such as charts, graphs, or maps

PRESENT

Share the findings of your analysis with others, such as stakeholders, customers, or partners, and explain what they mean and why they're important.

My toolbox:

Python

Python is my preferred language for data analysis due to its simplicity, readability, and strong library support, especially with tools like Pandas, SciPy, and NumPy for data manipulation and Matplotlib for visualizations, and web scraping tools like Beautiful Soup and Requests.

SQL

SQL is a vital skill for managing and querying relational databases, allowing me to easily work with large datasets through concise lines of code, and its versatility across platforms and database management systems make it a widely-accepted industry standard. As a data analyst, the ability to write efficient SQL queries and understand database design greatly enhances my ability to extract insights from data.

R

Its compatibility with Shiny also allows for interactive web application development, making R a flexible and powerful tool for data analysis..
